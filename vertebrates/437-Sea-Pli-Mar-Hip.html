Seahorse
Hippocampus ramulosus

 • Pliocene
• Marecchia River Formation
• Rimini, Italy

Size: 12 cm

Though I'm primarily an invertebrate collector, here's a beautiful fossil of a seahorse from a long-closed site in Italy. True fossil seahorses (genus "Hippocampus") are very rare in the fossil record, much more so than the similar-looking pipefish. Both belong to the Syngnathidae ("together+jaw"), a family of fishes named for and characterized by their fused, toothless jaws. Seahorses also posess flexible prehensile tails used for holding onto mates or substrates. H. ramulosus is actually a species that still exists today.

Measuring at 12 cm, this specimen is probably among the largest and best-preserved of its kind that I've ever seen.
