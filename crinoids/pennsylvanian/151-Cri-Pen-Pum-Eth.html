Parulocrinus sp.

 • Pennsylvanian
• Pumpkin Creek Limestone, Dornick Hills Group
• Oil Creek Outcrop, Johnston County, Oklahoma, USA

Size: 4.5 cm across

This specimen was originally labeled as Ethelocrinus sp. presumably on account of the prominent pustular calyx ornamentation. However according to Peter Holterhoff, Ethelocrinus necessitates a significant basal concavity formed primarily by incurving basal plates. I believe this specimen is more consistent with the definition of Parulocrinus, which has a flat base with no basal concavity. All of the definitive Parulocrinus I am aware of have smooth, unornamented calyxes, but I don't see why an ornamented form couldn't be included in the genus as defined by Strimple & Moore 1971.
