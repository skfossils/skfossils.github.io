Stellarocrinus virgilensis

 • Pennsylvanian
• LaSalle Limestone, Bond Formation
• Pontiac/Ocoya, Illinois, USA

Size: 5.5 cm across the arms

"Stellarocrinus virgilensis" is one of the more common constituents of the LaSalle crinoid fauna, but no less popular or beautiful for it. The genus exhibits very widely-spraddled/well-separated thick biserial arms along with a very shallow, highly-sculpted calyx, which give its crown a very distinct appearance. Its biserial arms also exemplify a trait of so-called "advanced cladids" in the Pennsylvanian, and a departure from the primitive cladid arrangement of uniserial pinnulate arms.

Officially, the ornamentation of the calyx and presence or absence of axillary spines on the arms distinguish the two species of Stellarocrinus found in the LaSalle Limestone. However I am not personally fully convinced of this distinction, because these features seem quite variable and intermediate forms seem to exist.
